project overview

We were tasked with choosing a accelerator startup from S22, W22, or W23 and designing an interface based purely off of the concept listed on the accelerator. In order to achieve this, we'd like to share our low-fidelity mockups and sketches that represent the imagined interface. Then, you will get to explore our high-fidelity mockups which took our low-fidelity mockups and brought them to life through Figma. In the final components of our work, we garned results from UserTesting to understand how effective and easily usable our interface is.

our startup selection: Koala

Koala is an all-in-one tool for online tutors! Its main goal is serve as an effective tool for tutors to engage with students who actively struggle with Zoom fatigue and online learning. In solving this problem, Koala equips tutors with educational games that engage students suffering from these conditions more effectively than traditional online teaching methods. In implementing this, Koala itself serves as not only a teaching space, but also a marketplace for tutors, parents, and students alike.

low-fidelity

After some thought, we were able to cohesively synthesize our 12 sketches into a low-fidelity replica that would represent three interfaces for the three types of users: students, tutors, and parents.

Based on the user type, the interface would need to have different components:

  • Log-in: a universal portal through which all users can enter the application

  • Dashboard: to view their upcoming sessions.
  • Session: where they will be engaging with the tutor in the interactive educational game.

  • Dashboard: to view their child's progress and to see their schedule.
  • Searching for Tutors: to sign their child up for more or different tutors.
  • Billing and Subscriptions: to view their subscription plan and pay any outstanding bills.

  • Dashboard: to view their schedule and launch any upcoming meetings.
  • Session: where they will be engaging with the student in the interactive educational game.
  • Games: where tutors can purchase games or subscriptions to use while engaging with their students
  • Students: where tutors can view and manage their students.
  • Schedule: garnering inspiration from Google Calendar, we wanted a layout that would mimic that mental model of a calendar for their schedule.

    user testing and analysis

    types of errors

    While feedback from peers and industry professionals were valuable, we were also able to take time to share our prototype with individuals who had no outside information about this work and were able to provide another set of perspective.

    Through UserTesting, we were able to have outside users test out our interface and provide us with valuable feedback. In our UserTesting sessions, we asked these testers to pretend that they were Koala online tutors, tasked with running their business successfully utilizing this platform. And overall, we expected our testers to be able to navigate through the interface and encounter what we thought were crucial day-to-day tasks that needed to be completed in order for an online tutor to successfully run their business: log in, start a meeting, check schedule, and prepare for upcoming sessions. All three testers performed in line with our expectations and completed the overarching task under 9 minutes while providing verbal feedback for further improvement on our interface.

    general performance on tasks

    Overall, the testers had little to no difficulty navigating the interface and landing on the pages we were directing them to. They also verbally communicated that they didn’t find the tasks difficult but were mainly confused about gaps in the interactive interface.

    potential interface changes

    Based on the feedback and results we received, some potential interface changes that we would make would include ensuring consistency amongst dates and information presented for all types of users. Furthermore, we may look into moving the “launch meeting” button under the student profile and having that be connected to the students’ information listed under “Upcoming Sessions.” This was suggested by one of the testers who was worried that she may be starting the wrong meeting. While our initial thoughts were that no tutor could have more than one session at a time, it may be in our best interest to clear up these connections with the “launch meeting” and put them in a place that is memorable and learnable to users.
